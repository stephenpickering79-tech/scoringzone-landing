The Problem

You Can Chip on the Range. But Can You Chip When It Counts?

Most golfers never practise under pressure. They hit the same shots from the same distance, to the same target, with nothing on the line. Then they wonder why they fall apart on the course. If you've ever wondered how to handle pressure in golf, the answer is simple — you have to train for it.

SIM Lab fixes this. Every test has a score. Every miss has a consequence. Every session makes you better at the only thing that actually matters — performing when it counts.

Why It Works

Why Pressure Practice Works

The gap between a good range session and a good round of golf isn't technique — it's pressure. Research in sports psychology consistently shows that skills practised under simulated pressure transfer to competition far more effectively than skills built in low-stakes environments.

When there's a score to beat, a timer running, or a consequence for a missed shot, your nervous system responds the way it does on the course. That's the entire point of SIM Lab.

The Chipping Proximity Gauntlet, the Morrish Short Game Challenge, the Elimination Round — these aren't just difficult drills. They're environments that force your technique to hold up when it's being tested. Most golfers never train this way, which is exactly why most golfers perform worse on the course than they do on the range. SIM Lab closes that gap.

SIM Lab Challenges

Competitive Golf Drills Built to Expose the Gaps

Competitive golf drills built to expose the gaps in your game — and close them.

Pressure Putting Test

Consecutive make requirements from multiple distances. Miss one and the pressure resets. The pressure putting drill that builds the focus needed to convert when a round is on the line.

Pressure Chipping Test

10 chips under tournament conditions — timer running, target zones scored, penalties for misses. Pressure chipping drills that replicate the feel of a chip-and-putt to save par.

Chipping Proximity Gauntlet

A multi-station distance control test that scores every chip on proximity to the hole. The tighter your misses, the higher your score. Builds the precision that turns chips into tap-ins.

Morrish Short Game Challenge

A comprehensive short game assessment covering varied lies, distances, and trajectories. One of the most demanding golf performance tests — designed to measure every dimension of your short game.

Century Drill

Score 100 points across a defined sequence of shots. Miss a target and points are deducted. Simple to understand, difficult to master — one of the most effective golf practice games.

Elimination Round

Advance through progressively harder target zones — one miss and you're out. The format that most closely simulates the pressure of competitive golf.
