The Problem

Why Am I Still Three-Putting?

If you've ever wondered why you're bad at putting despite hours of practice, the answer is usually the same: you're not practising the right way. Most golfers roll a few putts before a round, miss a couple, and call it a day. No structure. No targets. No feedback. And no idea how to stop three-putting when it actually matters.

The best way to improve putting isn't more repetition — it's the right kind of practice. Structured. Scored. Built to transfer to the course.

What Performance Based Practice Looks Like

A Smarter Golf Putting Trainer

Three steps. Real scores. Putts that hold up under pressure.

The best way to improve putting isn't more time on the practice green — it's the right kind of structured putting practice. Performance-based drills shift your sessions from mindless repetition to deliberate, measurable work that directly targets the skills that matter most on the course.

Scoring Zone is built around exactly this principle. Ladder drills develop putting distance control. Gate and string-line challenges train starting line accuracy. Pressure putting drills — like "make 10 in a row" or "miss one and start over" — build the mental toughness you need to stop three-putting when it actually counts.

Brad Faxon, the PGA Tour's all-time leader in putting average, put it simply: "The best drill is one where you can measure makes from different distances — consistency comes from chasing numbers." That's the entire philosophy behind Scoring Zone as a golf putting trainer.

Every drill tracks makes, exposes flaws in setup, stroke path, or tempo, and gives you instant feedback to correct them. The result is a golf putting practice app where every session produces data you can act on — improving speed control, starting line accuracy, and stroke repeatability in ways that transfer directly to the course.

Drill Library

Every Putting Situation. Covered.

From 3-footers to 40-foot lag putts — covering every putting distance control drill you'll need to lower your scores.

Ladder Putting

The essential putting distance control drill. Putt from 3, 6, 9, 12, and 15 feet — advancing only when you make three in a row. Builds the consistency that eliminates three-putts.

Lag Putting Drills

Two-putt from 20, 30, and 40 feet. Scored lag putting drills that build the touch to leave every long putt tap-in close.

Short Putt Drills

Make every putt inside five feet automatic. Consecutive make challenges that build the confidence to convert when it counts.

Gate Challenge

Putt through a narrow gate to train a consistent, on-line stroke. Immediate feedback on every rep — no guessing if you're starting it on line.

Pressure Putting Drills

Timed challenges, consecutive requirements, and penalty scoring. The pressure putting drills that replicate what you feel standing over a four-footer to save par.

Scoring Zone Putting Tests

Full assessments combining distance control, short putts, and pressure situations into one scored challenge. See exactly where your putting game stands.
