What Performance Based Practice Looks Like

A Better Way to Practise Chipping

Three steps. Real scores. Actual progress.

Scoring Zone is built around the way golfers actually get better. Research consistently shows that performance-based practice — hitting to targets, varying distances, and measuring outcomes — develops skill faster than working on technique alone. For anyone searching how to get better at golf chipping, the answer isn't more YouTube tips — it's structured, scored short game practice drills that transfer directly to on-course performance.

Dave Pelz, whose Short Game Bible remains the definitive text on the subject, makes exactly this point: data-driven, feedback-oriented practice that prioritises results over mindless mechanics is what separates improving golfers from ones who plateau. That's exactly what this golf chipping drills app does.

Every drill gives you a target score to chase, which creates the kind of mild pressure that sharpens focus and mirrors real on-course situations. Instead of thinking about your wrist hinge at the top or your weight shift, you're thinking about the flag — an external focus that lets your body execute naturally, the way it does when you're playing well.

The result is golf practice that's actually fun and builds adaptable, transferable skill rather than a swing that only works on the range. Variable lies, different wedge practice drills, scored outcomes — Scoring Zone doesn't just make practice more engaging, it makes it the best way to improve your chipping and lower your scores.
