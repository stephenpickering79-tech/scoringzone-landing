Why Your Short Game Practice Isn’t Working

You practise without a plan

The short game accounts for over 60% of all shots in a round of golf. Yet most golfers spend 80% of their practice time on the driving range hitting full shots. When they do practise their short game, there’s no plan — just random chips and putts until boredom wins. Without structure, you repeat the shots you’re already good at and avoid the ones costing you strokes. A golf practice plan app like Scoring Zone fixes this by building sessions around your actual weaknesses, not your comfort zone.

You can’t measure what you can’t track

Ask any golfer what their up-and-down percentage is. Most have no idea. Ask them their average putts per round. They might guess. Ask them where they lose the most strokes around the green — chipping? Bunker play? Lag putting? — and they’ll shrug. Without data, improvement is invisible. You might be getting better, but you can’t prove it. And when you can’t see progress, motivation disappears. That’s why tracking your golf short game stats is the single most important change you can make to your practice routine.

There’s no pressure in your practice

On the course, every shot matters. On the practice green, none of them do. That gap between pressure-free practice and high-pressure play is why so many golfers practise well but perform poorly. Your short game training needs to simulate the pressure of a real round — consequences for missing, scores that matter, and drills that force you to perform under stress. Scoring Zone’s pressure practice drills are designed specifically for this.

Putting Drills That Build Real Skill

Scoring Zone’s putting drills aren’t random. Each one targets a specific skill that translates directly to the course. Here are the drills you’ll use and why they work.

Ladder Putting — progressive distance control

Start at 3 feet and work your way out to 15 feet, making three consecutive putts at each distance before moving back. Miss and you stay at that distance until you make three in a row. This is the best golf putting drill for building confidence at every range. The consecutive requirement adds real pressure — just like on the course, where you only get one chance. Your score is the furthest distance you reach, and that number becomes the target to beat next session.

Lag Putting — eliminate three putts

Two-putt from 20, 30, and 40 feet. The drill scores your proximity on the first putt and whether you convert the second. This is the drill that kills three putts. Most amateurs three-putt because their first putt finishes too far from the hole — not because they miss short putts. Lag putting drills train the distance control that gets your first putt inside the three-foot circle every time.

Pressure Putting — simulate the moments that matter

Timed challenges, consecutive-make requirements, and penalty scoring that replicate the pressure of a real par-saving putt. These golf pressure practice drills are where your practice transfers to the course. When you’ve stood over a 5-footer in a timed drill with a streak on the line, the 5-footer on the 18th green doesn’t feel as scary.

Chipping & Wedge Drills That Transfer to the Course

Most chipping practice fails because it’s repetitive — same club, same distance, same lie. Scoring Zone’s chipping drills app forces variety, creativity, and consequence into every session.

Par in 2 — the up-and-down simulator

Chip and putt from different positions around the green. Each station is par 2 — one chip, one putt. Your score tells you your up-and-down conversion rate in real time. This is the closest you can get to simulating actual scoring situations without playing a round. Every chip matters because the putt that follows it matters. That chain of consequence is what makes this drill transfer to the course better than hitting chips into empty space.

One-Club Wizard — build feel and creativity

Chip to targets at 20 and 30 feet using only an 8-iron or 9-iron. No lob wedge allowed. This drill forces you to vary your technique — flight, spin, landing spot — instead of relying on one club for every shot. The best short game players on tour have multiple ways to play the same shot. This drill builds that versatility, and the scoring system rewards proximity over technique.

21 Points — pressure with consequences

Score points based on proximity. Reach exactly 21 to win. Go over and you bust — back to zero. This is one of the most popular scored golf drills in the app because the pressure builds with every shot. When you’re on 19 points and need a chip to finish within 3 feet, you’ll feel the same tension as a match-play situation on the course. That’s the point.

Track Your Stats — See Where You Lose Strokes

Scoring Zone doesn’t just track your practice — it tracks your golf short game stats round by round so you can see exactly where improvement is happening and where you’re still leaking strokes.

Up-and-down percentage

The single most important stat for short game performance. Your up-and-down percentage tells you how often you get the ball in the hole in two shots when you miss the green. Tour average is around 60%. Most amateurs sit between 20% and 35%. Tracking this number over time is the clearest measure of whether your short game is actually improving. Scoring Zone calculates it automatically from your round data and shows you the trend.

Strokes gained around the green

Strokes gained is the gold standard for measuring golf performance. It tells you how you perform relative to your handicap benchmark — not just your raw score, but whether you’re gaining or losing strokes compared to where you should be. Scoring Zone tracks strokes gained putting and strokes gained around the green separately, so you know exactly which part of your short game is holding you back. A golfer strokes gained app gives you tour-level analytics without needing a tour-level budget.

Putts per round and sand save rate

Scoring Zone also tracks putts per round, three-putt frequency, sand save percentage, and proximity to the hole from different distances. Each stat feeds into your overall short game picture. When you can open the app and see that your putts per round dropped from 34 to 31 over the past month, or your sand save rate climbed from 15% to 28%, you know the practice is working. That proof is what keeps you coming back.

A 20-Minute Session Walkthrough

Here’s what a real Scoring Zone practice session looks like. Twenty minutes, three phases, one clear score at the end.

Minutes 1–7: Putting warm-up — Ladder Drill. Open the app, start the Ladder Putting drill. Work from 3 feet out to 15, making three in a row at each distance. Your score is the furthest distance you reach. Last session you made it to 9 feet — today you’re chasing 12.

Minutes 8–14: Chipping challenge — Par in 2. The practice assistant has queued this because your up-and-down percentage dropped last round. Six stations around the green. Chip and putt from each one. Par is 12. You shoot 14. The app logs it, calculates your conversion rate, and adjusts tomorrow’s plan.

Minutes 15–20: Pressure finish — 21 Points. Chipping for proximity. Score points based on how close you get. Hit exactly 21 to win. Go over and you bust. You’re on 18 with two shots left. The pressure is real. You stick the chip to 2 feet. 21. Session over. XP earned. Personal best logged. Done.

That’s 20 minutes of structured, scored, pressure-filled practice. No wasted time. No guessing. Every shot tracked, every session building on the last one.
