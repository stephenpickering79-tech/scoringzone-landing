Best App to Improve Putting in 2026

What Actually Works

April 4, 2026 · 8 min read · Stephen Pickering

Key takeaway: The best putting app gives you scored drills with benchmarks, lag putting challenges for distance control, and pressure simulations that reset when you miss. Random practice on a mat won’t transfer to the course. Structured, scored sessions will.

Putting is the easiest part of your game to practise and the easiest to practise wrong. Most golfers on the practice green roll putts aimlessly with no target, no scoring, and no pressure. Then they wonder why it doesn’t transfer.

The best app to improve putting isn’t the one with the most videos or the prettiest interface. It’s the one that gives you structured, scored drills — and tracks whether you’re actually getting better. This article breaks down what a putting app needs to do, which ones deliver, and why dedicated putting practice outperforms anything built into a general golf app.

What a Putting App Actually Needs to Do

Scored drills with real benchmarks

Free practice builds comfort, not skill. The moment you add a score and a benchmark — “make 10 in a row from 5 feet” or “finish 8 of 10 lag putts inside 3 feet” — the practice becomes deliberate. Your attention narrows, your feedback loop shortens, and what you repeat is what you get on the course.

The best putting apps score every session. Not just fun scores for engagement — measurable performance against a meaningful benchmark. That’s the mechanism that turns practice into improvement.

Distance control, not just short-range makes

Short-range putts get all the attention, but distance control eliminates more strokes. Three putts almost always start with a poor first putt — one that finishes 6, 8, or 10 feet from the hole instead of inside 3. An app that only trains 3- to 6-foot putts is solving half the problem.

The best putting improvement apps include lag putting challenges explicitly — not as an afterthought, but as a core component. Distance control under pressure is a separate skill from making short putts, and it needs its own dedicated practice.

Pressure simulation

On the course, a 4-foot putt with money or a bet on it feels nothing like a 4-foot putt on an empty practice green. Apps that build in pressure — reset rules, streaks, time limits — train the part of putting that collapses under competition. It’s not a gimmick. It’s the gap between practice performance and course performance.

The Putting Drills That Actually Move the Needle

The Five-Foot Circle

Place a ball 5 feet from the hole. Make 10 in a row — miss any and the streak resets to zero. The 15-minute timer adds a time pressure element. This drill builds short-range confidence faster than any free practice session. Your score is the total putts taken to complete 10 consecutive makes — the lower, the better.

This is exactly the kind of drill most golfers skip because it’s genuinely hard. That’s why it works.

Lag King

Hit 10 putts from 40+ feet. Score how many finish within 3 feet of the hole. Focus entirely on speed — the line matters far less on a lag putt than the pace. Benchmark your score against your handicap level and track improvement over multiple sessions.

Golfers who improve their Lag King score typically see an immediate reduction in three putts on the course. The connection is direct and measurable.

The Deathstar Drill

Place 16 balls around the hole: from 3, 4, 5, and 6 feet — 4 from each distance. Make all 16 consecutively without a miss. Any miss and you reset to zero. A 30-minute time limit.

This drill is designed to build pressure tolerance across a range of short putts. The difficulty isn’t the distance — it’s sustaining focus and accuracy across 16 consecutive putts while the pressure compounds. Completing it cleanly requires the same mental state as holding a lead on the back nine.

How Apps Compare for Putting Improvement

General GPS and scoring apps (18Birdies, Golfshot, Hole19)

These apps record putts per round and show trends over time. That’s useful data — knowing your average puts per round is a starting point. But they don’t give you drills, benchmarks, or structured sessions to fix the number. They tell you the score; they don’t improve it.

Game-style apps (Break X Golf)

Break X Golf has multiple putting game formats and adds social competition to practice. The variety keeps sessions engaging, which matters for consistency. The gap: limited diagnostic data. You get a leaderboard score but not a breakdown of where your putting is breaking down or what to work on specifically.

Dedicated short game and putting apps

Apps built specifically for short game practice — with structured putting drill libraries, benchmark systems, and session tracking — deliver the most measurable improvement for golfers who practise consistently. The data feedback loop (drill score → trend → what to work on) is what makes the difference between app practice and effective practice.

What to Look for Before You Download

The short checklist

Before downloading any putting app, ask these four questions:

Does it have scored drills — not just games or videos? If there’s no scoring system and no benchmark, there’s no feedback.

Does it include lag putting specifically? Short-range drills alone won’t eliminate three putts.

Does it track session data over time? A drill score today is a number. Ten drill scores is a trend. You need both.

Is there pressure built into the drills? Reset rules, time limits, streak requirements. Practice that feels easy won’t transfer to the course when it’s hard.

If the answer to all four is yes, the app will improve your putting. If the answer to two or three is yes, it’ll help — but it won’t be the complete picture.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can an app actually improve your putting?

Yes — but only if it provides structured drills with scoring, not just entertainment. Apps that give you specific targets, benchmark your performance, and track improvement over multiple sessions have a measurable impact on putting. Random practice with no feedback doesn’t transfer to the course.

What causes most missed putts?

For short putts (inside 6 feet), face angle at impact is the primary cause — even a 2-degree error sends the ball offline. For longer putts, distance control is responsible for the vast majority of three putts. Both are trainable: face angle through stroke mechanics drills, distance control through lag putting challenges with scoring.

How long does it take to improve your putting with an app?

Most golfers who practise 15–20 minutes three times a week with scored putting drills see measurable improvement in 3–4 weeks. The fastest gains come from distance control — reducing the average lag putt proximity by even 1–2 feet eliminates most three putts.
