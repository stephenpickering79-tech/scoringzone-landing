Best Golf Apps in 2026: The Complete Guide

Practice Apps, GPS Apps, Stat Trackers — What’s Worth Your Time

April 8, 2026 · 5 min read · Stephen Pickering

The golf app market is enormous — and most of it is noise. There are hundreds of apps on the App Store and Google Play claiming to improve your game, lower your handicap, or give you “tour-level insights.” The reality? Most of them do the same thing: GPS yardages, digital scorecards, and a stat dashboard you’ll check twice before forgetting about it.

That’s not useless. Knowing your greens in regulation or putts per round is genuinely valuable. But there’s a gap in the market that most golfers don’t notice until they’ve downloaded five apps and still haven’t dropped a shot: tracking what happened and fixing what caused it are two completely different things.

This guide covers every major category of golf app — practice tools, GPS and rangefinder apps, stat trackers, putting-specific apps, and the head-to-head comparisons that actually matter. I’ve tested and written about each one individually. The links below take you to the full reviews.

If you’re short on time: the fastest way to lower your scores is structured short game practice with measurable benchmarks. That’s where Scoring Zone sits. But I’ll let you read the comparisons and decide for yourself.

Best Practice Apps

Practice apps are the category most golfers overlook — and the one with the biggest potential to actually move your handicap. A good practice app gives you structured drills, scores your performance, and tracks your progress over time. A bad one gives you a list of tips you could’ve found on YouTube.

Best Putting Apps

Putting accounts for roughly 40% of your total strokes in any given round. The average amateur three-putts three to five times per round — that’s six to ten shots you’re handing back before you even think about your swing. A putting-specific app needs to do more than count makes and misses. It needs to diagnose why you’re missing and give you drills that target the actual problem — whether that’s speed control, read accuracy, or pressure putts inside six feet.
