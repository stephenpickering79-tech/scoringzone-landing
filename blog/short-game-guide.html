Short Game

The Complete Short Game Guide

Chipping, Putting, Pitching — How to Practise the Part of Golf That Decides Your Score

April 8, 2026 · 5 min read · Stephen Pickering

What this guide covers: Every short game topic on the site — chipping, putting, pitching, practice routines, and scoring strategy — organised so you can find exactly what you need and start improving today.

Around 60% of your shots in a round happen from inside 100 yards. Chips, pitches, bunker shots, putts. That number barely changes whether you’re a 5-handicap or a 25-handicap. The short game is the majority of golf.

Yet most golfers spend the vast majority of their practice time hitting drivers and irons. Full swings feel productive. They’re satisfying. But they don’t move the needle the way 30 focused minutes around the putting green will.

This page is a central hub for everything I’ve written about the short game. Chipping technique. Putting drills. Practice plans you can actually follow. Scoring strategy that works on the course — not just in theory.

Pick the area where you’re losing the most shots and start there. Or work through it all. Either way, this is the guide.

Chipping and Getting Up and Down

Your up-and-down percentage is one of the biggest separators between handicap levels. Getting the ball close from around the green — consistently, under pressure — is a skill you can build with the right practice.

Putting is roughly 40% of your total strokes. Eliminate three-putts, sharpen your distance control, and learn to read greens properly — the combined effect on your scorecard is enormous.
