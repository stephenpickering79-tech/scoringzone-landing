Golf Short Game Tips & Drills

Putting drills, chipping techniques, and short game strategies to lower your scores.

Around 60% of every round is played inside 100 yards — yet most golfers spend the majority of their practice on the full swing. This blog is where we share the scored short-game drills, putting routines, chipping techniques, and honest app reviews that actually move handicaps. Expect data-led guides on average scores, handicap benchmarks by age and gender, side-by-side golf app comparisons (18Birdies, Golfshot, Arccos, Shot Scope, TheGrint, DECADE, Break X, and more), pressure putting tests, distance wedge work, and practice systems used by tour-level performers. New posts every week.

If you’ve plateaued in the 90s, you’re not alone — only about 25% of golfers ever break 90 and fewer than 5% break 80, and the reason isn’t your driver. It’s the up-and-downs you’re missing from 30 yards, the three-putts that quietly add 4–5 strokes per round, and the wedge yardages you’ve never measured. Every post here is written from that lens: stop guessing, start measuring, and put your practice time where it actually changes the scorecard.

You’ll find practical how-to guides (how to break 100, how to break 90, how to stop three-putting), honest reviews of the apps and training aids worth your money, head-to-head comparisons that cut through the marketing, and weekly drills you can take straight to the practice green or simulator. Whether you’re a junior just learning the short game, a mid-handicapper looking for the next 5 strokes, or a coach hunting structured practice frameworks for your students, the posts below are written for the way real golfers actually improve.
