Clutch Short Game Performances Steal the Show This Weekend

Monday, March 23, 2026

Five different tournaments, five clutch short game moments that decided champions. From DeChambeau's playoff heroics to Kim's wire-to-wire precision, this weekend proved once again that tournaments are won and lost within 100 yards of the pin.

This Week in Golf

DeChambeau's Playoff Magic Showcases Pressure Short Game

Bryson DeChambeau found himself in trouble on the 72nd hole, but his clutch par save from a difficult lie kept him alive for the playoff. When it mattered most in the extra hole, DeChambeau's approach from the rough set up a tap-in birdie that sealed the victory. The sequence highlighted how modern power players must still master the finesse game to close out tournaments. DeChambeau's ability to execute under extreme pressure, particularly his touch around the greens, separated him from a field of talented contenders. His short game has evolved significantly over recent years, becoming a weapon rather than just a safety net.

Scoring Zone Take: DeChambeau's clutch performance proves that even power players need elite short game skills when tournaments are on the line. Practice pressure situations regularly to build the mental toughness needed for crucial moments.

Source: PGA Tour

Fitzpatrick's 15-Footer Proves Putting Separates Winners

Matt Fitzpatrick stepped up to a crucial 15-foot birdie putt on the 72nd hole knowing exactly what he needed to do. The Englishman's stroke was pure, rolling the ball dead center for a victory that showcased his ice-cold putting under pressure. Fitzpatrick has built a reputation as one of the tour's most reliable putters, and this moment exemplified why. The victory margin came down to that single putt, highlighting how precision on the greens remains the ultimate differentiator in professional golf. Fitzpatrick's preparation and routine remained unchanged despite the enormous pressure of the moment.

Scoring Zone Take: Fitzpatrick's clutch putt demonstrates why consistent putting practice is non-negotiable. Focus on developing a repeatable routine that works under any pressure situation.

Source: European Tour

Gumberg's Up-and-Down Clinic Wins Tournament

Sarah Gumberg found herself in a challenging position on the final hole, needing an up-and-down to secure her first professional victory. Her delicate chip shot landed softly and trickled to within two feet, setting up a routine putt for the win. The shot showcased the hours of practice Gumberg has dedicated to her short game over the past year. Gumberg's short game transformation has been remarkable, turning what was once a weakness into her greatest strength. Her ability to get up and down from difficult positions gave her the confidence to play aggressively throughout the final round.

Scoring Zone Take: Gumberg's clutch up-and-down shows why mastering basic short game fundamentals pays dividends when it matters most. Regular practice of these pressure scenarios builds tournament-winning confidence.

Source: LPGA Tour

Kim's Wire-to-Wire Victory Built on Short Game Precision

Sei Young Kim navigated Sharon Heights' notoriously tricky greens with surgical precision throughout four rounds, never relinquishing her lead even with world #1 Nelly Korda breathing down her neck. Kim's short game performance around the challenging pin positions was masterful, particularly her ability to leave herself makeable putts from difficult angles. The Korean star's victory was built on consistent short game excellence rather than any single spectacular shot. Her ability to read the subtle breaks and slopes at Sharon Heights gave her a significant advantage over the field throughout the week.

Scoring Zone Take: Kim's wire-to-wire victory demonstrates how consistent short game excellence trumps occasional brilliance. Focus on building reliable fundamentals that perform under any conditions.

Source: LPGA Tour

Alker's 4-Footer Seals Champions Tour Playoff

Steven Alker faced a straight 4-foot putt to win his latest Champions Tour title in playoff conditions. The New Zealander's stroke was confident and true, finding the center of the cup for another clutch performance. Alker's late-career surge has been built on exceptional short game play and mental toughness in pressure situations. The putt capped off a remarkable sequence where Alker got up and down from a greenside bunker to force the playoff, then delivered when it mattered most. His short game consistency has made him one of the most feared competitors on the Champions Tour.

Scoring Zone Take: Alker's playoff winner shows how short putts require the same focus and routine as long ones. Practice those 'easy' 4-footers under pressure - they're often the hardest to make when tournaments are on the line.

Source: PGA Tour Champions

Drill of the Week: Chipping Pressure Test

Set up 5 balls around the green at various distances and lies. You must get each ball within 3 feet of the pin. Miss one, start over. This drill builds the mental toughness needed for clutch short game shots when tournaments are on the line.
